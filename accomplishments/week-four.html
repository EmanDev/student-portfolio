Week 4 Accomplishments

This page contains the work done for the fourth week of our On-the-job training at DMCI Masbate Power Corporation. You can check out the summary at the end of this page.

Week 4 is comprised of new sets of tours and field trainings within the power plant. Base from our Internship Work Plan, our fourth week of training contains new assignment based on power plant's electrical maintenance department.



Overview

Before we jump to the main part of this page, let us first describe each mentioned training done this week.

Generator Troubleshooting

  • Surge testing - to find weak coil-to-coil and phase-to-phase insulation.
  • Insulation testing - using megger, to determine the condition of electrical insulation.
  • These tests are important to conduct in a generator to determine whether the machine has deteriorated compared to its proper functional state.

Lighting Assembly and Motor Controls

  • Lighting: Fluorescent lamp and LED assembly.
  • Motor controls: Start-stop and forward-reverse.


Hands-on Training and Application

The students are now assigned at the Electrical Maintenance Department. For this stay, we should have prior knowledge about common things done in this department and we, trainees, should be able to do them/apply theoretical knowledge.


• Generator Troubleshooting •

Caterpillar 3 (CAT-3) generator troubleshooting. Base from the conducted test, multiple lines are shorted and before they can fully check some faults, according to the field electricians and supervising engineer, the generator must be heated first.


• Lighting Assembly •

Before moving to motor controls, we were first tasked to assemble fluorescent lamp and LED bulb. This is to test whether we know how to use basic electrical tools, how to splice, how to read schematic diagrams, and how to connect wires properly without shorting the lines.


• Motor Controls •

After our lighting assembly, we then proceed to motor controls. We were tasked to perform a start-stop, forward-reverse, and wye-delta motor simulation. We were provided with 3 magnetic contactors, basic electrical tools, spare wires, and power source.
