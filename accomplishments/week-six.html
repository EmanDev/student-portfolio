Week 6 Accomplishments

This page contains the work done for the fourth week of our On-the-job training at DMCI Masbate Power Corporation. You can check out the summary at the end of this page.

Week 6 is comprised of remaining tasks and assignments within the power plant. Base from our Internship Work Plan, our last week of training contains all remaining assignments based on power plant's electrical maintenance department.



Overview

Before we jump to the main part of this page, let us first describe each mentioned tasks done this week.

Pump Installation

  • Confirm new water pump replacement.
  • Install new water pump on the specified location.
  • Verify and double check installation and wire tapping of the said pump.

End of OJT

  • Discussion: Career preparations and End of OJT preparations.
  • Requirements: Comply documents such as Daily Time Record, Accomplishment report, and extras like photo ops & certification.


Application and OJT Completion

The end is near for the training of the students. We are tasked to install a new water pump to replace a faulty one. For the last part of this training, we will be complying required documents and will take photos for the completion and certifying the endless support given by our mentors, supervisors, and fellow workers through certificate of appreciation.


• Pump Installation •

One of the last tasks that were given to us was to properly install a water pump to be used to deliver demi-water to the vital thermal power plant accessories. The water pump was newly bought and it was easy to install with the contribution of each one of us.


• End of On-the-Job Training •

End of our On-the-Job Training. We, the students of Bicol University College of Engineering - Electrical Engineering Department, would like to thank DMCI Masbate Power Corporation for giving us the chance to experience what its like to work in a thermal and diesel power plant. We've learned so much from our seniors, share a few toast of snacks, and most importantly make friends with everyone at the site. Although we haven't been able to capture each momentos from the time we started upto the end, the experience that we gained from this training is enough to help us and motivate us in reaching our dreams and be a licensed Registered Electrical Engineer. Thank you DMPC Family! #SinoAko? #TeamDMCI! #TatakBUeño!
