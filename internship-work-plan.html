Internship Work Plan

Internship work plan is defined as the expectations for the experience of students formulated by the internship supervisor. This page contains a logical schedule and progression of work and the specifics of the work that the students will do.

Our internship training was divided into two. Since our internship requires a 240-hour long training (equal to 30 days), the first 15-day training (part 1) is for Power Plant Operations Control and the second 15-day training (part 2) is for Power Plant Electrical Maintenance Works.



Internship Job Descriptions

The following trainings will be conducted in a span of 30 days from 08:00 AM in the morning to 17:00 PM in the afternoon. Students are expected to learn, understand, and be able to pass any necessary assessment or exam at the end of the training.


• Duties and Responsibilities •

The students/trainees are expect to adhere to all company policies, rules and regulations for the entire duration of OJT. They must assist in the execution of all planned activities. Lastly, they are required to follow all instructions from their mentors/supervisors.

Power Plant Operations Control
Topic/s Skills being developed Duration Activities Tools to be Used Expected Outcome Status
Plant Operation Orientation Overview of Plant Operating System 1 day Orientation on power generation system to grid/substation Notebook & pen Able to understand how power plant supplied power to the transmission and distribution line, and household
Central Control Room Familiarization on Thermal Plant System (Boiler System, Turbine System, & Balance of Plant) 4 days Familiarization on how the thermal plant works per system (boiler, turbine, and balance of plant system) PC/Laptop, notebook, pen, radio handset and intercom Able to understand the function of all main auxillary equipment in all systems
Boiler Operation Knowledge on boiler operation and data monitoring 2 days Actual operation and monitoring of boiler such as periodic blowdown, soot blowing, coal feeders, and ash hoppers operations Basic hand tools, pen and paper

Able to understand and learn combustion process and draft system for steam boilers

Able to understand the best practice operation procedures

Able to learn routine operation and emergency procedures

Able to learn safety valve and burner management system
Turbine Operation Knowledge on turbine operation and data monitoring 2 days Actual operation and monitoring of turbine such as hourly reading of turbine parameters, sea water scraper operations, EDG operation and turbine oil filtration Basic hand tools, pen and paper

Able to understand and obtain knowledge regarding power plant operation using superheated steam

Able to obtain knowledge about different types of instruments and valves
Demi-Plant & ECP Plant Operation Basic knowledge on water treatment plant operation and water quality sampling 2 days Actual operation and monitoring of demi-water plant system and laboratory activities such as water sampling and lab assist Basic hand tools, pen and paper Able to understand and gain knowledge on how the water is treated that is being used by the whole plant
Control Room, Nigata, and High-speed Operation Knowledge on Nigata engine control system and main grid power system 4 days Load dispatching to DMTPP/MASELCO/NPC, recording or data gathering, starting and synchronizing of engines PC/Laptop, notebook, pen, radio handset and intercom

Able to operate Nigata and its auxiliaries as well as to monitor and interpret alarms and its parameters

Able to read and interpret single line diagram

Able to understand and execute load dispatching.
