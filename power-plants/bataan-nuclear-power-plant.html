Bataan Nuclear Power Plant, Morong, Bataan, Philippines

The Bataan Nuclear Power Plant is a large, imposing structure located on the Bataan Peninsula in the Philippines. It was built in the 1980s as a nuclear power plant, with the goal of providing a significant source of energy for the Philippines. The plant is situated on a 389-hectare site and is constructed of reinforced concrete, with a dome-shaped containment building designed to withstand earthquakes and typhoons. It was designed to have a generating capacity of 620 MW, which could have provided a significant portion of the country's energy needs.

However, the plant was never operational due to safety concerns, corruption, and mismanagement. Despite the significant investment in building the plant, it has remained dormant for decades and has become more of a tourist attraction.


TRIVIA

We were able to hold the presented fuel rod containing uranium and noticed that the uranium pellet was "floating" inside. Of course I was curious about how can this uranium pellet float? Well I asked about it but then I didn't get a good answer, and was ignored. Well, weeks have passed after the tour and while surfing the web, I also didn't get an answer. But then I stumbled upon this amazing tool and I've got the answer that I've been looking for the whole time.

Basically, uranium pellets do not float in their case, but rather, held in place by a combination of factors. The pellet is small, about 1cm in diameter and 1cm in height/length, thus having a gap within its case. This gap is filled with helium gas, which is introduced during the fabrication process. The pressure of the helium gas helps to keep the pellet centered within the fuel rod and prevents it from vibrating or moving around during operation. So there you go!
