Citicore Solar Farm/Citicore Clark Solar Power Plant, Mabalacat, Pampanga, Philippines

The Citicore Clark Solar Power Plant is a 25-megawatt solar farm that covers an area of approximately 50 hectares. It consists of more than 77,000 solar panels that are mounted on a single-axis tracking system, allowing them to follow the sun's movement and optimize their energy output. The solar power plant is expected to generate around 36,000 megawatt-hours of electricity per year, which can power up to 14,000 households. The project supports the Philippine government's goal of increasing the country's use of renewable energy sources and reducing its reliance on fossil fuels.

The Citicore Clark Solar Power Plant is just one of the many solar projects that Citicore is developing in the Philippines. The company is committed to promoting sustainable energy development and contributing to the country's efforts to mitigate climate change through the use of renewable energy sources.



Profile of the power plant

  • Located at Mabalacat, Pampanga
  • 25 MegaWatts Capacity
  • 6 years in operation (3 years by Indians and 3+ years by Citicore)
  • Equipped with 71,442 solar panels
  • Area is 25 hectares
  • Equipped with 18 inverters in 8 blocks

Overhauling Activities

Overhauling activities in the solar farm includes maintenance on the panels, electrical connections, and other electrical components such as OCPD and critical equipment being used.

  • Panel cleaning/washing
  • Removal of vegetation
  • Cable replacement
  • General troubleshooting of connection
  • Replacement of broken panels (seldom)

Most Critical Asset/s

As engineers and operators have stated, one of the most critical assets in the solar farm is the inverters and transformers. Reason being is that these equipments are much more expensive and causes huge downtime if broken/busted. Unlike the solar panels, they are not easily replaceable.

  • Inverters
  • Transformers
